Opportunity vs. Pressure — 27 Corridor Towns

Quadrant Summary

High opportunity · Low pressure
Marlinton · Davis · Parsons · Durbin · Bruceton Mills · Albright
Strongest existing commercial and infrastructure base. Front-of-house towns for the corridor. Marlinton is the standout: 5 mapped assets, 2 outfitters, municipal water.
High opportunity · High pressure
Cass · White Sulphur Springs · Hambleton
Real recreation draws but thin or fragile infrastructure. Cass has no municipal water. White Sulphur Springs sits on karst. These require care before scaling tourism.
Low opportunity · High pressure
Hillsboro · Aurora · Arbovale · Bartow · Huntersville · Harman
Most exposed, least to gain. Hillsboro and Aurora score 6 on pressure — both on karst, no municipal CWS, yet Droop Mountain and Cathedral SP pull visitor traffic.
Low opportunity · Low pressure
Kingwood · Terra Alta · Rowlesburg · Thomas · Masontown · Green Bank · others
Stable but largely outside trail-driven tourism scope. Municipal water, moderate business base, off karst.
